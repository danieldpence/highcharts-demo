Tinkering With Hightcharts

I wanted to get some experience using a javascript charting library, and after experimenting with a few other libraries, I came across Highcharts. This library is fairly straightforward in application, but highly customizable, as well. Here are a few examples of what can quickly, and easily be accomplished with the library.

I love the fact that the charts are interactive. This is especially useful when viewing time-series data over a significant period of time. Highcharts allows the user to highlight a section of the timeline to zoom in, and then the chart reacts. Pretty cool!

I also love the fact that Highcharts provides an easy way to print these charts. By clicking the hamburger menu icon, a user can print or save the chart down in a variety of formats. This is a super useful feature when it comes to sharing these charts in a business setting.
